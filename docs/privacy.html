Privacy Policy

PhoneRelay keeps mirroring local to your Mac and Android device.

This policy explains what PhoneRelay accesses, what it stores, and what it does not collect. Last updated: June 13, 2026.

Data Collection

PhoneRelay does not run an analytics service, advertising SDK, tracking SDK, or hosted user account system. The app does not sell personal information.

Screen mirroring

Video frames are used locally to show your Android screen in the Mac app. They are not uploaded by PhoneRelay.

Notifications

Notification forwarding is optional and off by default. If enabled, PhoneRelay reads Android notification text through adb and posts local macOS notifications.

Screenshots and recordings

Captures are saved to your Mac's Downloads folder when you use those controls. PhoneRelay does not send those files to a server.

Device metadata

The app may store local pairing details, device names, connection state, and preferences in macOS app storage so reconnects work.

Permissions

  • Local Network: used to discover and reconnect to Android devices on Wi-Fi.
  • Notifications: used only if you opt in to showing Android notifications on this Mac.
  • USB: used for adb communication with connected Android devices.
  • Downloads: used to save screenshots and screen recordings you create.

Support and Contact

For privacy questions or support requests, use the support page or open an issue in the public GitHub repository.
